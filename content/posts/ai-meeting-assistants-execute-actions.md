## What Is an AI Meeting Assistant That Executes Actions?

A traditional AI meeting assistant records, transcribes, and summarizes. That's table stakes in 2026. An **execution-capable** AI meeting assistant goes further:

- **Speaks during the call** — answers questions about your account, pulls CRM data, posts updates
- **Writes to your tools in real time** — files Jira tickets, drafts proposals, updates Salesforce *as the meeting happens*
- **Remembers across meetings** — builds a knowledge graph that compounds with every conversation
- **Owns a function** — dedicated agents for sales, engineering, recruiting, customer success

Think of the old guard as a tape recorder with a smart summary. The new guard is a **teammate who shows up, participates, and does the paperwork while you talk.**

## Step-by-Step: How to Set Up an AI Meeting Assistant That Executes for You

The old workflow was: attend meeting → review notes → take action. Here's how to flip that to: attend meeting → actions happen in parallel.

### Step 1: Choose your execution tier

Decide how much autonomy you want:

- **Full execution (Mina):** The AI speaks during calls, updates CRM, files tickets. Best for sales teams and high-volume customer calls.
- **Post-meeting automation (Fireflies/Circleback):** The AI captures action items and pushes them to connected tools after the call. Best for teams that want control over what gets automated.
- **Note-taking only (Otter/Read):** The AI produces summaries and action items. You still do the execution. Best for individual use or compliance-heavy environments.

### Step 2: Connect your execution stack

Whatever tool you pick, integration depth determines execution quality:

1. **Connect CRM first** — HubSpot or Salesforce is the backbone. Mina and Fireflies write directly. For Circleback, set up Zapier or Make automation.
2. **Add project management** — Jira, Linear, or Asana for ticket creation. Mina and Fireflies have native connectors. Circleback uses webhooks.
3. **Configure Slack/email** — for automated follow-ups and digests.
4. **Set up knowledge sources** — Google Drive, Notion, Confluence. The AI needs context to answer questions during calls.

### Step 3: Train your function agent (Mina only)

Mina's unique advantage is **function-aligned agents**:

- Create a **Sales Engineer** agent: upload product docs, connect CRM, configure MEDDPICC scoring. It will answer technical questions during discovery calls and update Salesforce after each answer.
- Create a **Scrum Master** agent: connect Jira, configure standup format. It joins sprint calls, captures blockers, and files tickets without anyone typing.
- Each agent has a job description and a success metric. Set your expectations once.

### Step 4: Test with a low-stakes call

Run a 15-minute internal meeting:

- For **Mina**: ask it a question about your account mid-call. Check that it speaks and that the CRM updates appear.
- For **Fireflies/Circleback**: check that action items appear in your project management tool within 5 minutes of the call ending.
- For **Otter/Read**: check summary accuracy and whether action items are correctly attributed.

### Step 5: Scale to your team

Once verified, roll out:

- Set auto-join for all calendar meetings
- Configure team-wide templates for summaries
- Share the "Ask your meeting AI" channel in Slack so the whole team queries past conversations

## Best For / Worst For

### Mina
- **Best for:** Sales teams running high-volume discovery calls; engineering teams with daily standups and sprint ceremonies; anyone who wants meetings to produce outcomes instead of follow-up work.
- **Worst for:** Solo users who only need transcription; teams with strict "no bots in meetings" policies (though bot-free mode exists).

### Fireflies
- **Best for:** Sales teams that need conversation intelligence (talk time, sentiment, objection tracking); teams at $10-19/seat price range wanting CRM integrations.
- **Worst for:** Teams needing real-time participation during calls; anyone who finds the visible "fred@fireflies.ai" bot intrusive in customer-facing meetings.

### Otter
- **Best for:** Educational use (lectures, interviews); individual professionals at $8-20/month; teams already using other AI tools via Otter's MCP server.
- **Worst for:** Teams needing execution, not transcription; multilingual teams (limited depth outside English).

### Read AI
- **Best for:** Zoom-standardized enterprises (Zoom Essential App = free premium); teams needing cross-channel summaries (meetings + email + messaging).
- **Worst for:** Teams needing action execution; small teams on the free plan (5 meeting cap/month).

### Circleback
- **Best for:** Quality-focused teams wanting excellent summaries and action items with Zapier/Make automation; YC-backed startups (Garry Tan endorsement).
- **Worst for:** Teams that need in-call participation; those wanting a single tool for both note-taking and autonomous execution.
