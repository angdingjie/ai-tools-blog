## What Is a Solopreneur AI Stack?

A solopreneur AI stack is a curated set of tools that covers every phase of your workflow — from creation and design to scheduling, automation, and business ops. The key word is *stack*: these tools work together through integrations, not in isolation.

A good stack covers five layers:

1. **Creation** — writing, coding, content generation
2. **Design** — visuals, UI, video, audio
3. **Management** — scheduling, project planning, client CRM
4. **Automation** — connecting everything so you don't have to
5. **Ops** — billing, accounting, document processing

The three stacks below are built from real tools with real pricing as of July 2026. Each includes a monthly cost estimate, integration notes, and the trade-offs you need to know before subscribing.

You build software alone. Your workflow is code → deploy → iterate. You don't need a designer, a project manager, or a content team — you need tools that multiply your output as a one-person engineering team.

### How It Works Together

You code in **Cursor** with agent mode handling multi-file refactors — describe a feature, Cursor proposes changes across 10+ files, you tab through and accept. When you need UI components, you prompt **v0.dev** for a shadcn-style card or table, paste it into your codebase, and iterate. **Claude Pro** handles your technical writing: API documentation, PR descriptions, and blog posts about your product. **n8n** (self-hosted on a $10/month VPS) ties it all together — auto-deploy from GitHub, trigger tests, post to your changelog. **GitHub Projects** keeps you organized at zero cost.

### Best For

- Indie hackers shipping MVPs in weeks, not months
- Freelance engineers who need to deliver full-stack features solo
- Developers who hate context-switching between tools

### Worst For

- Non-technical founders who can't self-host n8n or configure Cursor
- Developers who prefer terminal-only workflows (Cursor's GUI may feel heavy)
- Anyone building primarily on Windows without a Linux VPS

### Cost Breakdown

The **budget version** drops Cursor for **Windsurf Pro ($15)** and skips v0.dev by using Tailwind libraries directly — that's **$45/month**. The **premium version** adds **GitHub Copilot Pro ($10)** as a secondary autocomplete and **ChatGPT Plus ($20)** for brainstorming, totaling **$100/month**.

### How It Works Together

You start research in **Perplexity Pro** — it surfaces sources with citations, building your topic brief in minutes, not hours. **Claude Pro** takes the brief and writes your long-form content: newsletter essays, video scripts, SEO blog posts. **ChatGPT Plus** handles the short stuff — social hooks, Twitter threads, email subject lines — with DALL-E 3 for quick visual concepts.

**Canva Pro** is your design hub: thumbnails, social cards, banners. - People doing heavy video production with complex multi-track timelines
- Anyone who doesn't need video editing (Descript can be replaced by a free tool)

### How It Works Together

**HubSpot Free** holds your contact database — every call, email, and meeting logged against the client record. When a prospect books a meeting via **Calendly** (which syncs with HubSpot), you're ready before the call starts. During the call, **Notion AI** writes your meeting notes and auto-categorizes them into client folders.

After the call, **HoneyBook** generates a proposal with AI-written descriptions, sends it for e-signature, and creates the invoice when signed. **Wave** tracks expenses automatically — it pulls from your bank feed and uses OCR for receipt scanning. **n8n** (self-hosted) connects the pipeline: new Calendly booking → Notion note template created → HubSpot deal stage updated → reminder email sent.

### Best For

- Independent consultants juggling 5-15 active clients
- Coaches who spend too much time on admin
- Freelancers who need proposals out fast
- Agency-of-one operators handling sales + delivery solo

### Worst For

- Service providers handling over 50 clients (HubSpot Free caps at 1M contacts but gets unwieldy)
- People who need advanced email automation (add Lemlist at $32/month)
- Businesses requiring inventory or project billing (FreshBooks at $33/month would be better)

### Cost Breakdown

The **budget version** replaces HoneyBook with **Bonsai Starter ($29)** or manual Google Docs proposals and uses Notion free — that's **~$26/month**. The **premium version** adds **Motion ($34)** for AI scheduling, **Clay ($149)** for client research, and **FreshBooks Plus ($33)** for accounting, totaling **~$268/month**.

## Step-by-Step: How to Choose Your Stack in 5 Steps

### Step 1: Name Your Archetype

Be honest. You might be a Builder who also writes a newsletter, or an Operator who builds internal tools. Pick the archetype that describes your **primary revenue-generating activity**, not your hobbies.

- If you sell **code** → Builder
- If you sell **content** → Creator
- If you sell **your time/expertise** → Operator

### Step 2: Audit Your Current Subscriptions

Open your bank statement and list every recurring SaaS charge from the last 90 days. Most solopreneurs find 6-12 AI-related subscriptions they'd forgotten about. Sort them by: (a) essential for delivery, (b) nice-to-have, (c) unused.

Cancel everything in (c) immediately. Move (b) to a quarterly review.

### Step 3: Start with Free Tiers

Every stack starts with at least one free tool. For the Builder, that's **GitHub Projects**. For the Creator, **Beehiiv** handles up to 2,500 subscribers at no cost. For the Operator, **HubSpot Free** and **Wave** cover CRM and accounting.

Run your stack on free tiers for two weeks. If the free tier's limits hit your workflow (e.g., HubSpot's missing AI features, Beehiiv's included ads), upgrade one tool at a time.

### Step 4: Prioritize Automation

The biggest time leak for solopreneurs isn't tool choice — it's manual data transfer between tools. **Set up your automation layer first.** For the Builder, get n8n running on a $10 DigitalOcean droplet and connect GitHub → deployment → changelog. For the Creator, set up Make to route new blog posts → Beehiiv draft → Buffer queue. For the Operator, connect Calendly → HubSpot → Notion workflow so client onboarding is hands-off.

A single automation that saves 30 minutes per week pays for itself in a month.

### Step 5: Review Quarterly

The AI tool landscape changes fast. Set a calendar reminder every 3 months to re-evaluate:

- Are you actually using every tool in your stack?
- Has a better option launched at a lower price? (Midjourney's competitors are catching up fast.)
- Has your revenue model shifted? (A Builder who starts writing docs for other devs may need Claude Pro.)

## FAQ

### Can I mix tools from different stacks?

Absolutely. The stacks are starting points, not cages. Many solopreneurs are Builders who also run a newsletter — take the Builder code stack and add Beehiiv from the Creator stack. Just watch your total monthly cost. The recommended stacks stay under $150 because once you cross $200/month, you should evaluate whether you're earning enough from your tools to justify them.

### How long does it take to set up each stack?

The **Creator** and **Operator** stacks can be up and running in a weekend — most tools are cloud-based with drag-and-drop setup. The **Builder** stack takes longer (3-5 days) because self-hosting n8n requires configuring a VPS, DNS, and SSL. If that sounds painful, use **Make ($9/month)** instead of n8n and skip self-hosting entirely.

### What about free alternatives to paid tools?

- **Windsurf** (free tier) instead of Cursor Pro — unlimited basic completions, good for small projects
- **Canva Free** instead of Canva Pro — 250,000+ templates, just no AI image generation
- **Wave** instead of FreshBooks — free invoicing and accounting, just higher payment processing fees (2.9% + $0.60)
- **Obsidian** instead of Notion AI — completely free locally, AI via community plugins
- **Make** instead of Zapier — free tier gives 1,000 operations/month vs Zapier's 100

### Which stack saves the most time?

The **Builder stack** saves the most time in absolute terms (15-20 hours/week) because Cursor's agent mode eliminates the most rote programming work — boilerplate, tests, documentation, refactoring. The **Creator stack** saves more time *per output* — you can produce a 2,000-word newsletter in 2 hours instead of 8. The **Operator stack** saves the most time on the *administrative* side but has the smallest total time savings because service delivery itself (consulting calls, coaching sessions) can't be AI-automated.

### Will AI replace these tools next year?

The tools will evolve, but the categories won't disappear. What's Cursor today will have deeper agent autonomy tomorrow. What's Descript will integrate video generation.
