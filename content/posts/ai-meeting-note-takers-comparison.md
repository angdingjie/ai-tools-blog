## What Are AI Meeting Note-Takers?

AI meeting note-takers are software tools that join (or listen to) your meetings, transcribe everything said, and generate structured summaries, action items, and searchable transcripts. They eliminate the need to take notes manually — freeing you to actually participate.

In 2026, these tools fall into two architectural camps:

**Bot-based tools** (Fathom, Otter.ai) — The tool's AI assistant joins your calendar meetings as a participant. It listens, transcribes, and delivers notes automatically. You don't need to do anything beyond granting calendar access.

**Local capture tools** (Granola, Krisp 2.0) — These run on your device and capture the meeting audio from your speakers and microphone. They never appear as a meeting participant, which makes them more private and less intrusive. The trade-off is you usually need to have the app running.

The choice between them isn't technical — it's about your meeting culture, privacy needs, and how much control you want.

## Step-by-Step: How to Choose and Set Up Your AI Meeting Note-Taker in 10 Minutes

### Step 1: Decide — Bot or No Bot?

This is the single most important decision.

Ask yourself: *Would people in my meetings feel weird if they knew an AI was listening?*

- **Go bot-free** (Granola or Krisp 2.0) if you have sensitive client meetings, legal conversations, one-on-ones with direct reports, or any setting where a bot joining would feel invasive.
- **Go bot-based** (Fathom or Otter.ai) if your meetings are all-hands, team standups, or sales calls where everyone already expects recording. The bot handles everything automatically — no manual app setup per meeting.

### Step 2: Sign Up and Grant Calendar Access

All four tools ask for calendar access during onboarding. This is how they know which meetings to attend.

**For Fathom and Otter.ai:** Grant read access to your Google or Microsoft calendar. The bot will automatically join every meeting. You can blacklist specific recurring meetings (e.g., "weekly 1:1 with manager") in settings.

**For Granola and Krisp 2.0:** Calendar access is used to detect when a meeting is happening so the app can prompt you to start recording. The app still needs to be open on your device.

### Step 3: Configure Your Preferences

Each tool asks three configuration questions during setup:

1. **Summary length** — Short (bullet points), medium (paragraph summary), or long (full transcript with highlights)
2. **Action items** — Whether you want them auto-extracted and where they should be sent (email, [AFFILIATE: Notion], Slack, task manager)
3. **Sharing rules** — Automatically share notes with all participants vs. keep them private

Do this once and forget it. The tools are surprisingly good at respecting these defaults.

### Step 4: Test on One Real Meeting

Don't test on a fake meeting. Schedule a real 30-minute call and let the tool run. Here's what to check afterward:

- **Did the transcript capture everything?** Scroll to a part where two people talked over each other. Did the tool handle it?
- **Does the summary make sense to someone who missed the meeting?** Forward it to a colleague and ask.
- **Are the action items accurate?** Count how many were real next steps vs. hallucinated.

### Step 5: Fine-Tune Based on Results

Each tool has a "feedback" mechanism. If a summary was too long, you can train it to be shorter. If action items were missed, flag them. The AI improves quickly with feedback — within 3-5 meetings, most tools stabilize to your preferred style.

### Step 6: Set Up Your Integration Chain

This is where the real productivity gain lives. Don't just let notes sit in the tool — connect them to your workflow.

- **Fathom → CRM:** Automatically push meeting notes and action items to Salesforce or HubSpot after every sales call. This alone saves sales teams 2-3 hours per week per rep.
- **Granola → Notion:** Export meeting notes as pages in your team's knowledge base. Tag them by project for easy retrieval.
- **Krisp 2.0 → Slack:** Auto-post meeting summaries to a dedicated Slack channel so absent teammates catch up instantly.
- **Otter.ai → Google Docs:** Every meeting transcript becomes a shared Google Doc with comments enabled.

## FAQ

### Do AI meeting note-takers work with in-person meetings?

Yes, but only the local-capture tools. Granola and Krisp 2.0 use your laptop's microphone to capture in-person conversations. Bot-based tools (Fathom, Otter) can't join a physical room. For hybrid meetings with remote participants, all four work — the bot captures the audio or the local app records both room audio and remote audio.

### Are AI meeting notes secure enough for confidential meetings?

It depends on the tool and your configuration. Krisp 2.0 processes audio locally on your device — no data leaves your machine. Granola does cloud processing but doesn't appear as a meeting participant, so other attendees have no way of knowing it's recording. Fathom and Otter.ai both offer SOC 2 compliance and GDPR data residency options on enterprise plans. For legally privileged conversations, check your organization's policy — some still prohibit any AI transcription.

### Which tool has the best transcription accuracy?

Krisp 2.0 edges ahead at 96-98% accuracy, largely because its noise cancellation heritage means it captures cleaner audio to begin with. Fathom is a close second at 95-97% and is generally considered the most consistent across different speaking styles. Otter.ai and Granola both hover around 90-95% — acceptable for most purposes but noticeably worse with strong accents or overlapping speech.

### Can I use these tools with Microsoft Teams?

Yes — all four support Microsoft Teams. Fathom and Otter.ai join Teams meetings as bot participants. Granola and Krisp 2.0 capture Teams audio locally from your device. Krisp has the broadest platform support because it works at the OS audio level — it captures audio from any application that outputs sound, including Teams, Slack Huddles, Discord, Webex, and proprietary conference apps.

### Do I need to manually start recording every meeting?

Fathom and Otter.ai — no, if you've granted calendar access, the bot joins automatically. Granola prompts you to start with one click when it detects a meeting on your calendar. Krisp 2.0 requires you to start recording manually, though its calendar integration can auto-trigger recording for confirmed events. The trend across all tools is toward full automation — by the end of 2026, all four should offer "set and forget" modes.

## Conclusion

There is no single best AI meeting note-taker — the right choice depends entirely on how you work.

**If you value privacy above all else** and have meetings that shouldn't have a bot eavesdropping, get **Granola** (beautiful UI, great for Mac) or **Krisp 2.0** (superior noise handling, all-platform support).

**If you're on a sales team** where CRM hygiene matters more than anything, **Fathom** is the clear winner. Its two-way CRM sync and unlimited free tier make it the easiest recommendation for revenue teams.

**If you're on a tight budget** or need deep archival search across hundreds of past meetings, **Otter.ai** still holds its ground at $16.99/month.

**If you work in noisy environments** — coffee shops, co-working spaces, open offices — **Krisp 2.0** will give you the cleanest transcripts by a wide margin.

**My recommendation:** Start with Fathom's free tier. It's free, unlimited, and gives you a baseline for what AI note-taking should feel like. If you find the bot intrusive or need privacy, switch to Granola or Krisp 2.0. If you love it but want CRM sync, pay for Fathom Pro.
