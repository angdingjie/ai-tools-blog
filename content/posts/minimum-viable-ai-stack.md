You're paying for eight AI subscriptions. You use maybe three. Sound familiar?

The average knowledge worker now juggles 6-10 AI tools, yet survey data shows 70% of features go untouched. Tool fatigue is real — and expensive. At $20-50 per subscription, a full "AI suite" easily runs $150-300/month. Worse, jumping between tools kills the focused work they're supposed to enable.

The fix isn't a better tool. It's a framework for choosing the smallest working set that covers everything.

Here's the **Minimum Viable AI Stack (MVAIS)** : a decision framework for picking exactly four tools — one each for writing, research, organizing, and building — that cover 90% of a knowledge worker's needs for under $80/month.

## What Is a Minimum Viable AI Stack?

A minimum viable AI stack is the smallest set of AI tools that lets you write anything, research anything, organize anything, and build anything — without overlap, without unused features, and without context-switching friction.

The core insight is that knowledge work breaks down into four domains:

- **Write** — producing content, communication, and documentation
- **Research** — gathering, synthesizing, and analyzing information
- **Organize** — managing tasks, notes, projects, and knowledge
- **Build** — creating software, automations, and prototypes

Each domain needs exactly one primary tool. A tool that spans multiple domains is better. The goal is four tools maximum.

This isn't about finding the "best" AI tool in each category. It's about finding the tool that fits your actual workflow and eliminates the most adjacent subscriptions. A well-chosen stack compounds over time: the tools learn your patterns, the integrations get tighter, and you stop paying for things you don't use.

## MVAIS Framework vs. Traditional "Best Tools" Lists

| Dimension | Traditional "Top 10" Lists | Minimum Viable AI Stack Framework |
|---|---|---|
| **Goal** | Maximize features and options | Minimize tools while covering all needs |
| **Audience** | General / enterprise buyers | Knowledge workers, solopreneurs, indie hackers |
| **Selection logic** | Feature count, popularity, vendor marketing | Domain coverage, integration depth, overlap elimination |
| **Total cost outcome** | $150-300/month (10 tools) | $50-80/month (4 tools) |
| **Learning curve** | High — different UIs, shortcuts, workflows for each tool | Low — consistent interaction patterns |
| **Context-switch cost** | High — jumping between tools breaks flow | Minimal — fewer surfaces to navigate |
| **Tool overlap** | High — multiple tools doing the same thing (2 note apps, 2 meeting bots) | Zero — each tool owns one domain |

The difference is philosophy. Traditional lists ask "what's good?" The MVAIS framework asks "what's *necessary*?"

## Step-by-Step: How to Build Your Minimum Viable AI Stack

### Step 1: Audit your current AI subscriptions

Run a quick inventory. List every AI tool you're paying for, what domain it serves (Write, Research, Organize, Build), and how many hours per week you actually use it. Tools under 2 hours/week are candidates for elimination.

### Step 2: Pick your Write tool

Choose one tool for all writing: emails, documents, social posts, proposals, and editing.

**Best picks:**
- **ChatGPT Plus ($20/month)** — Best all-arounder. Handles long-form writing, editing, brainstorming, and formatting. The canvas mode is excellent for iterative document work.
- **Claude Pro ($20/month)** — Better for long-form, nuanced writing (whitepapers, strategy docs, detailed proposals). 100K-token context means you can drop in entire documents for editing.
- **Gemini Advanced ($22/month via Google One)** — Tied into Google Workspace. Best if you live in Docs and Gmail.

**Skip:** Jasper (too narrow), Copy.ai (redundant with ChatGPT), Writesonic (feature overlap).

### Step 3: Pick your Research tool

Choose one tool for gathering and synthesizing information: web research, competitor analysis, trend spotting, and data extraction.

**Best picks:**
- **Perplexity Pro ($20/month)** — Best-in-class for web-grounded research. Cites sources, handles follow-ups, and has deep search mode for thorough investigations. Replaces Google for complex queries.
- **ChatGPT with browsing** — If you already picked ChatGPT Plus, its browsing mode covers 80% of research needs. Perplexity is better, but using one tool beats two tools.
- **[AFFILIATE: Notion AI — $10/month add-on]** — If you're a Notion user, the AI layer turns your workspace into a research hub. It searches your own notes alongside the web, which is powerful for ongoing projects.

**Skip:**  Perplexity (if you already have ChatGPT Plus and rarely do deep research), Copilot (still catching up on research workflow).

### Step 4: Pick your Organize tool

Choose one tool for notes, tasks, knowledge management, and project tracking.

**Best picks:**
- **[AFFILIATE: Notion AI ($10/month add-on)]** — The gold standard for knowledge workers who want an all-in-one workspace. Notes, wikis, databases, project boards, and AI assistance in one place. The AI layer can summarize notes, generate action items, and surface related content.
- **Obsidian (free) + Smart Connections plugin** — For the privacy-minded. Local-first note-taking with graph view. The Smart Connections plugin adds semantic search without sending your data anywhere.
- **Linarr ($10/month)** — For task-heavy workflows. Think "Linear + AI." Built for people who live in project management rather than notes.

**Skip:** Evernote (stagnating AI features), Roam (steep learning curve), Mem.ai (not mature enough for production use).

### Step 5: Pick your Build tool

Choose one tool for automation, coding, and prototyping.

**Best picks:**
- **[AFFILIATE: Cursor ($20/month)]** — Best AI-first code editor. Agent mode can build entire features from a description. Works for actual developers AND non-coders who want to prototype.
- **GitHub Copilot ($10/month)** — Best if you're a working developer who wants AI assistance within your existing VS Code or JetBrains setup. Less autonomous than Cursor but deeply integrated.
- **Zapier AI ($30/month)** — For automations and no-code workflows. Connects 6,000+ apps. The AI layer generates entire Zaps from natural language descriptions.

**Skip:** Bolt.new (limited to frontend prototypes), Replit (good for learning, weak for production work), v0.dev (Vercel-only, too narrow).

## Best For / Worst For

**Best for:** Solopreneurs, indie hackers, remote knowledge workers, small teams (<10 people), and anyone experiencing AI tool fatigue. The framework shines when you need to reduce monthly spend without losing capability.

**Worst for:** Enterprise teams with compliance requirements (they'll need dedicated enterprise tools), engineers specializing in ML/AI (need specialized infrastructure tools), people who enjoy trying every new tool (this framework assumes you value focus over novelty).

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use free tiers instead of paid subscriptions?**
Most tools offer generous free tiers. ChatGPT's free tier (GPT-4o-mini) is fine for light use. Perplexity's free tier has 5 Pro searches per day. Obsidian is fully free. You can start with free tiers and upgrade as your usage grows.

**Q: Do I need all four tools if I don't code?**
Replace Cursor with Zapier AI or skip the Build domain entirely. Your stack becomes ChatGPT ($20) + Perplexity ($20) + Notion AI ($10) = $50/month. That covers writing, research, and organization.

**Q: What about AI for meetings and email?**
Tools like Fireflies.ai and [AFFILIATE: Fathom] (free for individuals) are single-purpose and don't replace a core domain. Use Fathom for free meeting notes and integrate them into your Organize tool. Email AI is built into ChatGPT/Claude — you can paste email chains and ask for summaries.

**Q: How do I handle images and design?**
Midjourney, DALL·E, and Canva AI are nice-to-haves, not core stack tools. Subscribe only if you regularly create visuals. Most text tools generate basic diagrams and charts without a separate subscription.

**Q: Will I miss out on new tools if I commit to a stack?**
The MVAIS framework isn't a permanent commitment. Re-evaluate every 6 months. But the mental overhead of switching tools constantly costs more than the $5/month you might save by jumping to a cheaper alternative.

## Conclusion + Your Next Step

Tool fatigue is the silent productivity killer of the AI era. Every new subscription adds a surface — more UIs to learn, more shortcuts to memorize, more tabs to manage. The Minimum Viable AI Stack isn't about getting the best tool in every category. It's about getting the *fewest* tools that cover your actual work.

Start today: audit your subscriptions. Cancel anything under 2 hours/week. Pick one tool per domain. Run this stack for 30 days. You'll save money, reduce cognitive overhead, and — counterintuitively — get more done with less AI.

**Your next step:** Export your current AI subscriptions from your payment processor (Stripe, PayPal, credit card statement). Map each one to Write / Research / Organize / Build. Identify overlaps. Cancel everything except your four picks. Then set a reminder for 30 days to review.
