## What Is the "AI Stack" Problem?

The "AI stack" is the collection of AI tools a knowledge worker pays for monthly. For solopreneurs, it typically includes a general-purpose chat AI (ChatGPT, Claude), a research tool (Perplexity), a coding assistant (Cursor, Copilot), a writing helper, and maybe a voice dictation tool or scheduling assistant.

The problem isn't any single tool — it's that most of them **overlap in capability**. ChatGPT can write and research. Perplexity can summarize and brainstorm. Claude can write and code. Gemini has a million-token context window and attaches to your Google Drive. When every tool tries to be an everything tool, your budget bleeds.

In 2026, the market has matured enough that a **focused stack of two to three purpose-built tools** outperforms a scattered collection of five-plus. The key is matching tools to your actual job functions, not buying what's trending.

## Step-by-Step: How to Audit Your AI Stack in 30 Minutes

You can replicate this audit right now. Here's the exact process I followed.

### Step 1: List Every AI Tool You Pay For

Open your billing portal (Apple subscriptions, Stripe, credit card statements) and list everything. Don't forget annual plans, add-ons, or "free trials" you forgot to cancel.

### Step 2: Categorize by Job Function

For each tool, answer: *What is the one job I primarily use this for?*

Your categories should look like:
- **Writing & editing** (long-form content, emails, proposals)
- **Research & analysis** (cited sources, data gathering)
- **Coding & development** (writing code, debugging, automation)
- **Voice & dictation** (speaking instead of typing)
- **Scheduling & admin** (calendar, email triage, task management)
- **Design & visuals** (presentations, social media graphics)

### Step 3: Identify Overlapping Tools

This is where the savings are. Mark every tool that serves the **same job function** as another tool. For example:
- If you have both ChatGPT and Claude, ask: *Can Claude do everything I use ChatGPT for?* (For most writing/coding users, yes — Claude Opus 4.6 scores higher on both prose quality and SWE-bench Verified.)
- If you have both Perplexity and ChatGPT/Claude with web search: test whether the built-in search passes your accuracy bar. For 80% of research tasks, it does.

### Step 4: Apply the 2-Hour Rule

Parallel Labs' framework nails this: **if a task consumes less than 2 hours per month and doesn't directly generate revenue, it doesn't deserve its own subscription.** Tools covering low-frequency tasks should be dropped or replaced with free alternatives.

### Step 5: Calculate Your ROI per Tool

For each tool, estimate:
- Hours saved per month × your hourly rate = monthly value
- Subtract subscription cost = net ROI

If a tool saves you 5 hours/month and you bill at $100/hr, that's $500 in value. A $20 tool is a steal. A $20 tool saving 30 minutes? Cut it.

### Step 6: Consolidate and Cancel

Select the **minimum viable stack**: one writing/coding tool, one research tool (if needed), one automation/scheduling tool. Cancel everything else.

## FAQ

### How did you pick the $50/mo threshold?

$50/month is the "pain threshold" for most solopreneurs — the point where subscription spend feels significant but still rationalized. The average solopreneur spends $86/mo, so cutting to $50 saves $432/year. Below $50, you start making meaningful compromises. The goal is to hit the price point where **every tool earns its keep.**

### Can I go lower than $50/month?

Yes. ### Should I cancel ChatGPT entirely in 2026?

Not necessarily. Keep ChatGPT Plus if you rely on DALL-E image generation, voice mode, or ChatGPT's real-time browsing with Bing. But if your primary use is writing and coding, [AFFILIATE: Claude Pro] delivers better results for the same price — and won't show you ads.

### Which AI tool has the best free tier in 2026?

Gemini. Google offers the free tier with 1 million tokens of context and Gmail/Docs integration — no other company matches that generosity. DeepSeek V4 is also completely free but has data privacy concerns. Claude's free tier is solid for occasional use but limits you to lower-tier models.

### How do I know if I'm overpaying for AI tools?

Two red flags: (1) you have more than three paid AI subscriptions, or (2) you can't immediately tell which tool does which job without checking. Run the 2-Hour Rule from Step 4 above — if a tool doesn't save you at least 2 hours per month on a revenue-generating task, it's costing you money, not making it.
